## Description  (max 4000 chars)
```
Save My Snaps is an independent tool that rebuilds the photos and videos from the data export you download from Snapchat. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Snap Inc.

When you download your own data, your saved photos and videos come back with their captions, stickers, and drawings stored as separate overlay files. Save My Snaps composites those overlays back onto the original media, so your memories look the way you remember them.

HOW IT WORKS
1. Request and download your data export from the service you saved your memories in.
2. Open Save My Snaps and select the export folder or archive — no account or login required.
3. Review your photos and videos and pick the ones you want to keep.
4. Save them to your Photos library, share them, or export to Files.

PRIVATE BY DESIGN
Everything happens on your device. There are no accounts, no servers, and no network uploads — your photos and videos never leave your phone. The app contains no analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.

BUILT FOR REAL LIBRARIES
- Captions, stickers, and drawings rebuilt onto both photos and videos
- Handles large exports with thousands of items
- Resumes automatically if you switch apps or a long export is interrupted
- Save to Photos, share to other apps, or export to Files

Your memories are yours. Save My Snaps helps you get them back — complete, private, and offline.

"Snapchat" is a trademark of Snap Inc. Save My Snaps is an independent application and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Snap Inc. The name is used only to describe the file format this app can read.
```

## Trademark / review notes

- **"snapchat" is deliberately left out of the Keywords field.** Using a
  competitor's trademark as an ASO keyword is the least-defensible use (it's for
  discoverability, not description) and Apple may reject it under its metadata
  rules. Keeping it out is the lower-risk choice. *Trade-off: it reduces search
  discoverability for people typing "snapchat".* If you decide the discoverability
  is worth the risk, adding `snapchat` costs 9 chars and fits within the limit —
  but do it knowingly.
- **Nominative fair use** (the legal basis for the text references) generally holds
  when: you use only as much of the mark as needed, you don't use their logo/font/
  styling, and you don't imply sponsorship. The description above is written to
  stay inside those lines, with an explicit disclaimer.
- The disclaimer paragraph at the end of the Description mirrors the one in the
  app's Terms of Use and the footer of every docs page.
- Biggest remaining exposure is the **icon** (see warning at top), not the text.
